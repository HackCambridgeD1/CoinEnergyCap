About: Cryptocurrency Energy Usage

Methodology

We are using the methodology suggested in [1] to calculate a lower bound for the energy demand based on the hashrate of a crypto currencies network and a reasonable hash efficiency (energy/hash). We are basing the efficiencies on results from [2], who calculated the lower bound for Bitcoins energy usage by taking the state of the art mining devices in 2020. This data was only tabulated for about 15 of the largest coins, for all other coins, we make reasonable assumptions and guess an appropriate rate based on the existing ones. We obtain Hashrate, Price and Volume from the API provided by Minerstat [3] and the market cap from Coinmarketcap [4]. Estimates for the energy usage of the UK and global energy intensities come from government sources [5][6][7]. We have assumed that one needs to plant about 6 trees to compensate for 1 ton of Carbon Dioxide. We believe this to be a reasonable number, after having seen figures from 1-30 for the same. Last, to calculate the "rounds around earth" one could drive with a car, we have selected an electric car, which should take about 15kWh/100km on which we base our calculations. We do note that our data may be incorrect, in particular for sources that are not listed in the table for source [2].
